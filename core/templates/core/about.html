What is MTSA?

MTSA (Masai Technical Student Association) voting system, as the name suggests, is a web application that provides a voting platform for the students. The system can be interacted with either as an organizer (university staff) or as a voter (university students).

The organizer is responsible for the creation of polls (objects in the system that represent actual elections), and to register contending candidates to the polls. The organizer can close a poll once the election is over. This will ensure that no further voting continues on the poll.

The voter is responsible for casting votes on polls created by an organizer. The voter interacts with the ballot-box (a system object that represents an actual ballot box) to create a vote for their preferred candidate. A voter can only vote once on any poll.

Both the organizer and the voter can access poll results and generate poll reports at any point as long as the organizer has not deleted the poll. The aim of having results accessible to both parties at any time is to promote transparency, fairness and accountability on this digital democracy platform.

Why is MTSA voting system

The Masai Technical Training Institute management had the following reasons to create this platform.

  • For election organizers, planning elections is synonymous with high costs: you need to find an appropriate physical space to hold the election, inform eligible voters, prepare voting forms and organize the vote count. However, with an online voting system, you have maximum flexibility and efficiency in election planning and ballot creation as it is done online. Observe voter turnout in real-time and engage voters with a few mouse clicks.

  • Paper-based voting is both cost-intensive and burdensome for the environment. Conduct an environmentally friendly election and save paper, printing and delivery costs by shifting online. Additionally, save on personnel and time expense and skip the drawn-out manual vote count process by using an online voting system.

  • By eliminating the use of physical ballots and manual vote counting you can avoid result-distorting mistakes such as loss of voting documents and miscounted votes. Automatic vote counting with MTSA voting system allows you to access results during and after the election. Reports can also be generated.

  • Online election security measures target system availability, elimination of potential manipulations, as well as compliance with data protection and security. MTSA voting system uses mathematical and cryptographic methods in adhering to fundamental democratic principles.

  • Voting should be simple, location-independent and accessible. This system aims to reduce barriers to participation and offer eligible voters secure online voting in order to boost turnout. Adding the option to vote online allows eligible voters to cast their votes at any time within the election period and from any location.
